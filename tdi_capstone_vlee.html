I. Definition¶

1. Project Overview:¶

  1. Objective:

    • Predict the M&A event occurrence of the US public company based on the company information and CEO's biographic and psychological attributes.
      • For CEO's psychological attributes, I will identify the risk-taking nature of CEO of the NYSE Public companies, using Regulatory Focus theory.
      • Examine if CEO's regulatory focus impacts on his/her decision making on the company event such as company acquisitions. (i.e., "M&A")

  2. Audience:

    • 1.1. Institutional Investor who would like to get supplementary analysis on the future M&A possibilities of their invested companies/equities
    • 1.2. Individual Stockholders of the company who wants to forecast company action/event that may impact on the company value and earning shocks.

2. Problem Statement¶

  • Research Question: Does CEO's regulatory focus(i.e, psychological character towards risky behavior) impact on his/her decision on company action with high risk, such as mergers and acquisitions (M&A)?
  • Hypothesis: CEO with promotion regulatory focus (i.e., rist-taking psychological attribute) is more likely to decide M&A, comparing to CEO with prevention regulatory focus.

Based on CEO's verbiage choices used in their letters to shareholders in the company annual reports, I will classify CEO's psychological attributes in to two regulatory focus classes: (1) promotion focus and (2) prevention focus. Once I classify CEO regulatory focus and create regulatory foci variables, I will find the relationship between CEO's regulatory focus and occurence of M&A, along with other variables, such as CEO compensation/biographic information and company infromation that may potentially impact on CEO's M&A decision.

3. Metrics¶

  • Company information:
    • ln_firmsize: Natural log-transformed company size (Total Revenue, collected from WRDS)
    • Previous M&A records:
      • acq_ct: total counts of previous acquisitions a company had made between 1996 and 2016
      • acq_val: log-transformed total acquisition values (i.e., deal values in USD millions) a company had spent in M&A between 1996 and 2016
      • acq: M&A occurence binary variable (1, if a company had previously acquired other companies, 0, if a company never had acquisition.)
    • Sector information of the company: My final data had total 13 sectors: 'Finance', 'Capital Goods', 'Transportation', 'Consumer Services', 'Unknown', 'Health Care', 'Technology', 'Public Utilities', 'Energy', 'Miscellaneous', 'Consumer Non-Durables', 'Basic Industries', 'Consumer Durables'
  • CEO Biographic information:
    • age: CEO's age
    • net_income: CEO's net income
    • bonus: CEO's compensation bonus
    • restricted stocks: CEO's restricted stocks
  • CEO Psychological Attributes:
    • Regulatory Focus:
      • prom_pct: total counts of promotional words term-frequency(TF score) appeared in the CEO's shareholder letter, comparing to the promotion regulatory focus dictionary, which includes total 25 promotional regulatory. I double-counted the same vocabulary to calculate the percentage. For example, if "motivation" was the promotional regulatory focus word listed as a key in the regulatory focus dictionary and CEO used this word twice in his/her letter, I added two, instead of one to count the promotional regulatory focus vocabs term frequency.
      • prev_pct: total counts of promotional words term-frequency(TF score) appeared in the CEO's shareholder letter, comparing to the promotion regulatory focus dictionary, which includes total 25 promotional regulatory.
      • regfoci: If CEO's prom_pct is higher than prev_pct, I gave a dummy variable 1, and classified this CEO as the CEO with promotional regulatory focus and vice versa.
      • regfoci_score: prom_pct - prev_pct

II. Analysis¶

Data Exploration¶

CEO shareholder's letter data¶

I collected total 7367 CEO shareholder's letters from 10-K annual reports listed at http://annualreports.com for 973 publicly traded companies in the U.S. between 1996 and 2016. Using NLTK, I created corpus for each company by year and counted the term-frequency(TF) used for the promotion focus words and prevention focus words used by the CEOs in their letters. Using this TF matrix for promotion focus words and prevention focus words respectively, I calculated the the percentage of promotion/prevention focus words against total promtion/prevention focus words in the dictionary used by the CEO per letter.

CEO compensation data¶

I collected CEO compensation data including CEO net income, bonus, restricted stocks and salary from Wharton's WRDS database.

Previous Company M&A records¶

I collected historical M&A records for 973 publicly traded companied in the U.S. between 1996 and 2016 from SDC M&A database and caclulted log transformed M&A deal values in $USD millions spent by each company, total count of past acquisitions by company. Based on the M&A information given in this data set, I created a binary dummy variable tells the past M&A occurence of the company. If the company had ever acquired another company, I gave "1", otherwise, "0".

Other Company Information¶

I collected company sector information for 973 publicly traded companied in the U.S. between 1996 and 2016 from SDC M&A database and firm size (i.e., total revenue of the company) from the WRDS CEO biographic information Acceess Relational Database, using SQL.

Polynomial Features Regression Model¶

Should I allow for interactions between my categorical variables? (company sector information)

While a scatter plot above with sector variable as x-axis and acquisition label as y-axis above, I did not find any concave or curvy line that may be explained well with polynomial features. Therefore, I decided not to use a polynomial features/polylnomial regression. Before I confirm this decision, I created a linear polynomial regression model with degree 1,2,3, repectively and compared thier R2 and RMSE to see training data points' model fiteness.

from sklearn.preprocessing import PolynomialFeatures

def create_polynomial_regression_model(degree):
    "Creates a polynomial regression model for the given degree"
    poly_features = PolynomialFeatures(interaction_only= True, include_bias = False, degree=degree)
  
    # transforms the existing features to higher degree features.
    X_train_poly = poly_features.fit_transform(X_train)
  
    # fit the transformed features to Linear Regression
    poly_model = LinearRegression()
    poly_model.fit(X_train_poly, y_train)

    # predicting on training data-set
    y_train_predicted = poly_model.predict(X_train_poly)

    # predicting on test data-set
    y_test_predict = poly_model.predict(poly_features.fit_transform(X_test))

    # evaluating the model on training dataset
    rmse_train = np.sqrt(mean_squared_error(y_train, y_train_predicted))
    r2_train = r2_score(y_train, y_train_predicted)

    # evaluating the model on test dataset
    rmse_test = np.sqrt(mean_squared_error(y_test, y_test_predict))
    r2_test = r2_score(y_test, y_test_predict)

    print("The model performance for the training set")
    print("-------------------------------------------")
    print("RMSE of training set is {}".format(rmse_train))
    print("R2 score of training set is {}".format(r2_train))

    print("\n")

    print("The model performance for the test set")
    print("-------------------------------------------")
    print("RMSE of test set is {}".format(rmse_test))
    print("R2 score of test set is {}".format(r2_test))

Model Selection/Validation/Evaluation¶

A logistic regression model/Polynomial linear regression/CART regression model were trained on approximately 7,400 US public companies using company attributes of sector firm size and CEO biographic attributes of age and net income, and CEO's regulatory focus in promotion focus and prevention focus.

CEO's regulatory focus was classified in two categories: promotion focus and prevention focus. CEO's regulatofy focus score was computed based on following steps:

  1. Count term frequency(TF) of promotional words and preventional word used in the the shareholder's letter written by CEO. (I double counted the redundant word useage)
  2. Calculate the % ratio of TF of CEO's promotion/prevention focus words over total promotion focus words/prevention focus words registered in the promotion/prevention regulatory focus dictionary.
  3. was calculated based on the term frequency of each regulatory words used by the CEO in the shareholders' letter comparing to the total counts of each regulatory focus words in the dictionary.

Logistic Regression Model The train and test accuracies was around 64% (with a classification threshold of 0.5)—thus, overfitting does not appear to be a notable concern.

AUC-ROC (Area Under the Receiver Operating Characteristics) when trained on the train/test dataset was about 0.69.

Polynomial Lienar Regression Model RMSE for Polynomial Linear Regression Model with degree of 1 to 3 was around 0.42 to 0.50. R2 score of them was around -0.01 and 0.20.

CART model With 10-fold cross validation, I found CART model as the best predictive model, per cross validation score in negative MSE. CART regression's average precision and recall are around 0.74 and 0.75. Average F1 score is 0.74 for predicting M&A occurrence. The maximum depth of the tree is 9; this hyper parameter is searched through GridSerch CV.

AUROC score is about 74% - thus, overfitting issue is not a severe concern with this model.

Features:

  1. % regulatory focus word useage: 2. Firm Sector : 3. ln_Firm Size: 4. CEO Age: 5. CEO Net Income
  2. CEO Net Income
  3. CEO compensation information
    'ceo_age', 'net_income', 'salary', 'bonus', 'restricted_stocks',
  4. CEO regulatory focus score: 'regfoci_score', which followed prom_pct - prev_pct.
  5. Log-transformed company size in USD Mils: 'ln_firmsize'
  6. Company sectors (13-1 sector to avoid multicollinearity): 
            'Capital Goods', 'Technology', 'Transportation', 'Unknown',
        'Consumer Durables', 'Consumer Non-Durables', 'Consumer Services',
        'Energy', 'Finance', 'Health Care', 'Miscellaneous', 'Public Utilities'

Label: Binary classification of historical M&A occurence of the company. 0, if no M&A occurred in the past. 1, if M&A occurred before. "M&A occurrence" simply means the company had acquired other companies before.

Example Prediction Results: A CEO of the US public company in {%s}sector with {%d} firm size, who is {%d} years old, earns {%d} net income and has more {%s} regulatory focus may have {%d}% chance of making a company acquisition decision.

Conclusion: The prediction made by this model is trained/based on a specific set of CEO biographic and psychological attributes and Companies' general attributes. However, there are a number of other exogenous factors such as macro economic situation, event-driven shocks, and company's business objective that can affect company acquisition ocrruence.

Please consider this prediction only as the supplementary analysis on the future company M&A occurence predictions.
